# Venue Selection Checklist

**Rule:** Submit to **ONE** venue at a time. Simultaneous submission is unethical.

Pick your primary target before final formatting. Score each 1–5 (5 = best fit).

## Comparison Matrix

| Criterion | IEEE Access | IEEE Conference (ICBC/INDIN) | Islamic Fintech Journal (IMEFM) | Blockchain: R&A (Elsevier) |
|-----------|:-------------:|:----------------------------:|:-------------------------------:|:----------------------------:|
| IEEEtran template fit | 5 | 4 (6–8 pp limit) | 2 (different template) | 2 (elsarticle) |
| Blockchain + evaluation | 5 | 5 | 3 | 5 |
| Halal / Islamic angle | 3 | 3 | 5 | 3 |
| Open access | Yes (APC) | Usually no | Hybrid | Hybrid |
| Review speed | ~6–10 weeks | ~8–12 weeks | ~8–16 weeks | ~8–12 weeks |
| Page limit | Flexible | 6–8 pages | ~8000 words | Flexible |
| Impact / indexing | SCIE, Scopus | Varies | Scopus, ESCI | SCIE, Scopus |

## Option A: IEEE Access (Recommended for full paper)

**Pros:**
- Accepts full system + evaluation papers
- IEEEtran journal format matches our baseline
- Broad readership, open access visibility
- No strict page limit

**Cons:**
- Article Processing Charge (APC) ~USD 1,995 (check current rate)
- High volume journal — strong novelty statement required

**Fit:** Best if you have testnet evaluation data (Section VI-B) and want maximum reach.

## Option B: IEEE Conference (e.g., IEEE ICBC, IEEE TrustCom)

**Pros:**
- Faster feedback cycle
- Good for prototype / early results
- Can extend to journal later (must disclose prior publication)

**Cons:**
- 6–8 page strict limit — must compress ethics + design sections
- May require travel/presentation
- Double-blind options vary

**Fit:** Best if testnet eval is delayed and you want conference feedback on design.

## Option C: International Journal of Islamic and Middle Eastern Finance and Management (IMEFM)

**Pros:**
- Strong fit for Maqasid / halal trust framing
- Reviewers understand Islamic ethics context

**Cons:**
- Requires different LaTeX template (Emerald/Elsarticle)
- Less blockchain-native audience
- Must emphasize finance/halal governance angle

**Fit:** Best if Islamic ethics is the primary contribution, blockchain is secondary.

## Option D: Blockchain: Research and Applications (Elsevier)

**Pros:**
- Topical fit for blockchain protocol papers
- Good Scopus indexing

**Cons:**
- Must convert from IEEEtran to elsarticle
- Competitive acceptance rate

**Fit:** Best if evaluation is strong and you can invest in template conversion.

## Recommended Path

1. If testnet eval ready within 4 weeks → **IEEE Access**
2. If eval delayed → **IEEE Conference** short paper first, then extend to IEEE Access
3. If ethics/halal angle is primary story → **IMEFM** (convert template)
