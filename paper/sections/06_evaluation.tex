\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}

We structure evaluation into two tiers aligned with deployment maturity: (VI-A) qualitative analysis and functional validation available on local Hardhat networks today; and (VI-B) quantitative Base Sepolia benchmarks pending funded testnet deployment.
This separation avoids conflating developer-machine measurements with public network costs~\cite{casino2021systematic}.

\subsection{Qualitative Analysis and Functional Validation}
\label{sec:eval-a}

\subsubsection{Security Properties}
Table~\ref{tab:security} maps design requirements from Section~\ref{sec:prelim} to observable protocol behaviors validated by unit tests and manual scenario runs.

\begin{table}[t]
  \caption{Security properties and validation method.}
  \label{tab:security}
  \centering
  \footnotesize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{3pt}
  \begin{tabularx}{\columnwidth}{@{}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.34\columnwidth}X@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Property} & \textbf{Validation} \\
    \midrule
    Immutability of rejection & \texttt{verifyBatch} on Rejected reverts \\
    Revision traceability & \texttt{parentBatchId} preserved; parent reason readable \\
    Role separation & Non-producer cannot register; non-auditor cannot verify \\
    Batch identity & Invalid \texttt{batchId} reverts on all mutators \\
    Revocation integrity & Only Verified $\rightarrow$ Revoked permitted \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Immutability.}
Once \texttt{rejectBatch} executes, \texttt{verifyBatch} on the same identifier reverts with \texttt{InvalidStatus}.
Scenario~C (below) confirms that repeated verification attempts leave on-chain state unchanged---supporting \emph{Amanah}.

\paragraph{Revision traceability.}
\texttt{registerRevision} creates a new \texttt{batchId} while storing \texttt{parentBatchId}.
Consumers inspecting a verified revision can navigate to the rejected parent and read its \texttt{rejectReason} and original \texttt{ipfsCid}, supporting audit transparency.

\paragraph{Access control.}
OpenZeppelin \texttt{onlyRole} modifiers gate mutating functions.
Tests assert that unauthorized addresses cannot register, verify, reject, or revoke, addressing basic privilege escalation concerns in the v1 prototype.

\subsubsection{Functional Scenarios}
Three end-to-end scenarios from \texttt{docs/EVALUATION.md} were executed on Ethereum Sepolia testnet (contract \texttt{0xDaCA688e86F438A7cD6B0C9B69606C67CE85Dc92}) on 2026-06-08, with evidence archived in \texttt{docs/evaluation/}.

\paragraph{Scenario A (happy path).}
Scenario~A (batch \#1): producer registration and auditor verification on Ethereum Sepolia testnet; consumer page at \texttt{/verify/1} displays verified status.

\paragraph{Scenario B (rejection and revision).}
Scenario~B: batch \#2 rejected, revision batch \#3 verified with parent link preserved.

\paragraph{Scenario C (tamper resistance).}
Scenario~C: \texttt{verifyBatch} on rejected batch \#2 reverts (\texttt{InvalidStatus}); on-chain state unchanged.

Functional validation on Ethereum Sepolia confirms protocol correctness and wallet-free consumer reads via the Next.js frontend; gas costs on L1 testnet upper-bound L2 deployment feasibility~\cite{optimism2024spec}.
