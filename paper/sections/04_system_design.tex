\subsection{Batch Lifecycle}
Batch status follows a finite state machine: Pending $\rightarrow$ Verified $|$ Rejected; Verified $\rightarrow$ Revoked.
Rejected batches cannot be re-verified; producers submit a \emph{revision} as a new batch linked via \texttt{parentBatchId}, preserving audit history.

\subsection{Smart Contract Specification}
Contract \texttt{HalalChain.sol} (Solidity ${\hat{}}0.8.20$, OpenZeppelin AccessControl~\cite{openzeppelin2024access}) defines:

\textbf{Roles:} \texttt{PRODUCER\_ROLE}, \texttt{AUDITOR\_ROLE}, \texttt{DEFAULT\_ADMIN\_ROLE}.

\textbf{Batch struct:} producer, productName, ipfsCid, createdAt, status, auditor, auditedAt, auditIpfsCid, rejectReason, parentBatchId.

\textbf{Key functions:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{registerBatch(productName, ipfsCid)} --- new batch, status Pending
  \item \texttt{registerRevision(parentBatchId, ipfsCid)} --- revision after rejection
  \item \texttt{verifyBatch(batchId, auditIpfsCid)} --- Pending $\rightarrow$ Verified
  \item \texttt{rejectBatch(batchId, reason, auditIpfsCid)} --- Pending $\rightarrow$ Rejected
  \item \texttt{revokeBatch(batchId, reason)} --- Verified $\rightarrow$ Revoked
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Off-Chain Storage}
Documents are pinned on IPFS via Pinata; only CIDs are stored on-chain~\cite{benet2014ipfs,saad2020ipfs}, minimizing gas while preserving content-addressable integrity.
