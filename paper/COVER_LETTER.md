We submit the manuscript *HalalChain: A Trustless Batch Traceability Protocol on Base L2 Integrating Islamic Ethical Design Requirements with Immutable Audit Trails* for consideration in IEEE Access.

## Novelty and contribution

1. **Minimal three-role RBAC protocol** for UMKM halal batch traceability on Base L2, separating producer registration, auditor verification, and wallet-free consumer reads.
2. **Maqasid-aligned design requirements** mapped to enforceable on-chain properties (immutability, role separation, revision traceability).
3. **Open-source implementation** (Solidity, Hardhat tests, Next.js dashboards) with reproducible evaluation on Base Sepolia testnet.
4. **Consumer accessibility**: QR-based verification without cryptocurrency wallets, lowering adoption barriers for end users.

## Fit with IEEE Access scope

The manuscript addresses blockchain-enabled supply chain traceability, smart contract security, and Layer-2 cost efficiency—topics within IEEE Access's computer science and engineering scope. The halal certification trust gap in Indonesia provides a concrete, socially relevant application domain.
